The Big Squeeze

The Big Bang wasn't an explosion. It was a squeeze. What you see as a single point is a whole dimension you can't see into. Here's the proof — no math, just a zoom.

Try the trick once

Look at your hand. It's a whole thing. Now zoom in: it's billions of cells. Each cell is a whole thing. Zoom in again: each cell is billions of molecules. Each molecule is a whole thing. Keep going down — atoms, then quarks, then whatever the next layer is — and the same trick keeps working.

Every "whole" is made of "points." Every "point" is itself a whole you couldn't see into. That sentence is the entire paradigm. The rest of this page is just walking the ladder until you see what it implies.

The squeeze

You just climbed twelve steps. At every single one, the same trick worked: what looked like a whole from one side looked like a point from the other. There was never a step where the trick stopped. So why would it stop at the universe?

It doesn't. The universe is one more step on the same ladder. It is a whole made of galaxies — and it is a single point inside a higher dimension you can't see directly. What you call the Big Bang — that "single point everything came from" — is the back of that point. You are looking edge-on at a higher dimension's interior. From your angle it looks like one infinitely small dot. From its own angle, it is vast.

That's why the universe appears to be expanding. You are not watching an explosion spread outward. You are watching what it looks like to be inside something whose shape you cannot perceive from outside. The "expansion" is the inside view of a dimension that has always had its full extent. It isn't a Big Bang. It's a Big Squeeze, and you are inside it.

Black holes are the same trick

A black hole is the same mechanism, happening locally, that you can point a telescope at. From outside, all you see is a single point — a "singularity." Same words physicists use for the start of the universe. That is not a coincidence.

Inside that point is not "infinite density." Inside is a whole dimension, squeezed into what looks to us like a dot because we are observing it from a direction that cannot see in. Every black hole is a Big Bang seen from the outside. Every Big Bang is a black hole seen from the inside. Same squeeze, different vantage. The universe contains uncountable Big Bangs. We're just inside one of them.

One recursive unit

Stop here and notice what the ladder did. There was no special floor (quarks were just where our instruments stop). There was no special ceiling (the universe is just where our light stops). At every step the rule held: a point is a hidden whole, a whole is a hidden point. The pattern doesn't care about scale. It doesn't care whether you're looking at an atom or at everything that exists.

That is what the paradigm calls a recursive unit: a single rule that produces every layer by being applied to its own output. You don't need a separate physics for atoms and a separate physics for galaxies. You need one rule, used over and over, with the observer's position deciding which layer they happen to be calling "the whole" today.
